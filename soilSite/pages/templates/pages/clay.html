  • Clay/Chalky Soil

    Very Alkaline providing poor conditions for growing plants. They are suitable for vegetable growth but are poor for fruits.

    PH - 7.1-8.0

    The poor water and nutrient-retention means chalky soil can be extremely dry during hot weather, requiring frequent watering to keep the ground moist. That, combined with its high alkalinity, makes chalky soil nutrient-poor since plant roots are unable to absorb the locked-up iron and manganese.

Lettuce

It is ideal to have lettuce grown in soil with high water retention, as lettuce needs an ample supply of water to grow.

Radishes

Radishes are great space plants as they can be grown without natural lighting and are easy to grow. In addition they thrive in soil with high iron content which are found on extraterristrial planets

Tomatoes

Tomatoes are ideal to grow if minerals such as magnesium and calcium are present which boost the growth of tomatoes
